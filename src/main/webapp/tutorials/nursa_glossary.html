Glossary:

TERM DEFINITION
26S proteasome A large protein complex comprised of a core 20S proteasome, PA28alpha, and ubiquitin. Plays a major role in the proteolytic recycling of cellular proteins.
AF-1 Amino-terminal region of many nuclear receptors that mediates ligand-independent activation of transcription; shown to synergize with AF-2 in some receptors.
AF-2 Carboxy-terminal region of many nuclear receptors that forms part of the receptor LBD; undergoes a ligand-induced conformational change required for the recruitment of coactivators; shown to synergize with AF-1 in some receptors.
bHLH/PAS Basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH)/Per-Arnt-Sim (PAS) domains.These domains mediate homodimeric and heterodimeric interactions between proteins.
Coactivators Factor(s) that interact with and enhance the activity of transcriptional activators.
Corepressors Factor(s) that interact with and enhance the activity of transcriptional repressors
Coregulators Factors recruited by nuclear receptors that potentiate or attenuate signaling by those receptors.
CoRNR box A signature motif of NCoR and SMRT which contains a hydrophobic core variously determined as FXXFF or LXX I/H I XXX I/L, similar to the LXXLL motif (NR box), characteristic of nuclear receptor coactivators.
DBD DNA-binding domain: a zinc finger-based motif characteristic of members of the nuclear receptor superfamily that mediates their interaction with DNA hormone response elements.
HDACs Mammalian histone deacetylases comprise three classes based on sequence homology. Class I contains yeast Rpd3-like proteins HDAC1, HDAC2, HDAC3 and HDAC8; class II contains yeast Hda1-like proteins HDAC4, HDAC5, HDAC6, HDAC7, HDAC9 and HDAC10; and class III contains the yeast Sir2-like proteins.
Helix 12 The most C terminal of a series of 11 or 12 alpha-helices elucidated from the LBD structures of several nuclear receptors. It realigns in the presence of ligand to form a hydrophobic cleft, composed of helices 3, 5, and 12, which binds the LXXLL motifs of coactivators.
Homeobox Homeobox (hox) transcription factors have been shown to regulate cell fate and segment identity by promoting the expression of specific genetic programs. In contrast to their restricted biological action in vivo, however, most homeodomain factors exhibit promiscuous DNA binding properties in vitro, suggesting a requirement for additional cofactors that enhance target site selectivity.
HRE Hormone response element
LBD Ligand-binding domain; an extended region in the carboxy-terminal portion of many nuclear receptors that mediates interaction with ligands..
LXXLL motif (NR box) An amphipathic alpha-helical motif with an LXXLL core (where L is leucine and X is any amino acid) that recurs from a single to multiple copes in many coactivators; contributes to ligand-dependent interactions of many coregulators with AF-2 domain of nuclear receptors.
Nuclear localization signal The selective transport of proteins through the nuclear pore complex of eukaryotic cells is performed by their nuclear localization signals (NLSs). The first NLS was identified in the SV40 T antigen as a short cluster of five contiguous positively charged residues in the sequence PKKKRKV.
SERMs Selective estrogen receptor modulators; molecules which bind with high affinity to the estrogen receptor (ER) but exhibit tissue-specific effects distinct from 17-beta estradiol, acting as estrogen agonists in some tissues and as antagonists in others. Examples include tamoxifen, clomiphene and raloxifene.
Sin3A A corepressor for the Mad-Max heterodimer and a homolog of the yeast global-transcriptional repressor Sin3p; interacts with nuclear receptor coregulators NCoR, SMRT and TGIF
