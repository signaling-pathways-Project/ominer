In the early 1990s, some investigators such as Keith Yamamoto had suggested a role for non-DNA ''nuclear acceptor'' molecules. Evidence for the existence of these factors of these factors came from experiments in Pierre Chambon’s laboratory in which “squelching”, or competition for a common limiting factor, was observed between coexpressed receptors in reporter assays in mammalian cells.
